# DeepReadingEmpathy
An essay on the cost of Internet-addicted brains.

## Abstract
Internet-habituated brains don't read, they scan. Years of skimming and hyperlink micro-decisions have physically reorganized the neural pathways deep reading depends on, with EEG research showing screen reading activates brain states closer to daydreaming than focused analysis. The cost isn't just attention. MRI studies show the brain rehearses real social and emotional scenarios while reading fiction, and losing that habit measurably weakens empathy, a loss the essay connects directly to political tribalism and the erosion of democratic patience. Recovery is possible: twenty-to-thirty-minute undistracted sessions, phone in another room, active annotation, treating focus as a physical skill in rehab rather than a setting you can toggle.
